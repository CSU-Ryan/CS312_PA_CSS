Peppers are one of the most widely used spices in the world. They come in a stunning variety of colors, shapes, and heat levels. From the mild bell pepper to the scorching Carolina Reaper, there is a pepper for every palate. Capsaicin is the chemical compound responsible for the burning sensation when consuming hot peppers. Peppers are native to the Americas and were spread around the world after the Columbian Exchange.

  • Bell Pepper
  • Jalapeño
  • Habanero
  • Ghost Pepper
  • Carolina Reaper

Peppers are rich in vitamins A and C. They are used in cuisines worldwide, from Mexican salsas to Indian curries to Italian arrabbiata sauce.

Pepper Facts

  • The word "pepper" comes from the Sanskrit word pippali.
  • Christopher Columbus mistakenly called them peppers because of their spicy taste, confusing them with black pepper.
  • Over 50,000 varieties of pepper plants exist worldwide.
  • The Scoville scale measures the heat of peppers in Scoville Heat Units (SHU).
  • Bell peppers have 0 SHU, while the Carolina Reaper exceeds 2 million SHU.
